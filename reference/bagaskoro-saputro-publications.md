## 1. High-Performance Electric Two-Wheeler Fast Charger Based on Intelligent Control Algorithm

**Authors:** Subiyanto S., Rizky Ajie Aprilianto, Mario Norman Syah, **Bagaskoro Saputro**, Abdurrakhman Hamid Al-Azhari, Nektar Cahayasabda, Bayu Adi Pambudi, Faiq Mananul Faqih, Icha Arifah Annisa, Dwi Bagas Nugroho, Siva Khaaifina Rachmat, Dewi Anggriani

**Journal:** *Journal of Automation, Mobile Robotics and Intelligent Systems (JAMRIS)*, 20(2), 175-184

**Year:** 2026

**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.14313/jamris-2026-030

**ISSN:** 1897-8649 (print), 2080-2145 (online)

**Topic:** EV fast charging, interleaved buck converter, PI-FLC control

**Relevance:** Directly related to hybrid off-grid solar charging station. Proposes an interleaved buck converter (IBC) with PI-FLC algorithm for fast charging of electric two-wheelers (E2W). Achieves 0-100% SoC in 57.75 minutes (vs. 180 min PID CC-CV and 240 min PI CV). Uses NMC 72 V / 20 Ah battery at 1C rate. Ripple cancellation reduces output filter capacity enabling compact design suitable for off-grid stations.

## 2. Ray Tracing-Based Modeling of Bifacial Photovoltaic Systems in Greenhouse Agrivoltaics

**Authors:** Endang Widiyawati, Subiyanto, Siti Ridloah, Budi Sunarko, **Bagaskoro Saputro**, Rizky Ajie Aprilianto, Mario Norman Syah, Abdurrakhaman Hamid Al-Azhari, Deyndrawan Sutrisno, Aisya Fathimah, Apriansyah Wibowo, I Gede Bagus Jayendra

**Journal:** *Jurnal Teknik Pertanian Lampung (Journal of Agricultural Engineering)*, 15(2), 510-524

**Year:** 2026

**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.23960/jtepl.v15i2.510-524

**Topic:** Bifacial photovoltaic modeling, ray tracing, agrivoltaics

**Relevance:** Related to hybrid solar energy systems. Develops ray tracing-based optical and electrical model for bifacial PV in greenhouse environments. Incorporates Uniform/Non-Uniform rear-irradiance distribution, LambertW function-based electrical modeling. Demonstrates 25-30% rear-side energy capture improvement. Optimal checkerboard pattern configuration provides 5% higher performance vs. 35° tilt.
